About

Born and raised in Richmond, Virginia. I went to school at Virginia Tech to study Biology, Physics and Chemistry. Upon graduating, I worked there for both the College of Science and the Registrar’s Office building websites and setting up small Linux print servers using CUPS. I began my work at Capital One as a contractor in January 2013, and was hired as a full time employee at the end of December in 2014.

I’m a firm believer in giving back to one’s community. It has been my privilege to work with CodeVA (non-profit) in Richmond to teach kids how to write code and build robots for competitions in middle and high school. Additionally, I partner with the Collegiate School in Richmond working with high school students on building a robot to compete in the annual FIRST competition. We’re also actively working on neural nets, autonomous robotic drive systems, a Farmbot installation and more.

For creative release, I find balance as an amateur woodworker and brewer. I build household furniture and cabinets using exotic woods and new age techniques. As of December, 2017 I’ve brewed 12 batches of beer, the latest of which is a Christmas Ale. Keep up with us over at our blog.

I’m also fairly involved with my church, Hill City. They are an incredible community of Christians who’s mission is to provide others with a safe place to explore their faith and ask questions; a community that I am honored and proud to be a part of.
